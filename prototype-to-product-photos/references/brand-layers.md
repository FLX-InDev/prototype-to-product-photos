# Brand Layers

Define optional brand layers here before using this skill for a repeatable brand workflow. A brand layer adapts scene design, styling, and photography mood while the product locks in `SKILL.md` remain authoritative.

## What Brand Layers Control

A brand layer may control:

- setting type and room character;
- palette relationships around the product;
- props, furniture, art, florals, and styling density;
- editorial mood and campaign tone;
- lighting, camera grammar, crop preferences, and negative space;
- scene treatments to avoid.

A brand layer must not control:

- product category or semantic use;
- component count;
- construction details;
- intrinsic product color;
- material behavior;
- dimensions;
- required or forbidden product placement.

## Selection Rules

- If the user specifies a brand, use exactly one matching brand layer from this file.
- If the specified brand is missing, ambiguous, or not defined here, ask for clarification before generation.
- If the user does not specify a brand, use `Neutral / default`.
- Do not blend multiple brand layers unless the user explicitly asks for a collaboration, mixed campaign, or comparison series.
- User-provided campaign notes may narrow a selected brand layer, but they may not override product fidelity, semantic-use, construction, or material locks.

## Neutral / default

Use this layer when no brand is specified. Create an authentic, collected French residential setting chosen from the approved scene lanes in [shot-recipes.md](shot-recipes.md). Keep the palette responsive to the original product while preserving its intrinsic color. Use crafted furniture, layered natural materials, refined light, intentional soft furnishings, curated objects, and controlled negative space. Do not imitate any defined brand's distinctive language.

# Product-specific hard rules

Apply the matching category section after building the product-region map and locks. The factual description may narrow or explicitly override a default placement rule. It may not silently override visible construction or appearance in the prototypes.

## Bedding and blankets

### Bedding

- Keep sheets, fitted sheets, duvet covers, pillowcases, shams, bedspreads, and other bedding on a bed unless the description explicitly permits another support.
- Preserve the exact component inventory and count. Verify every component separately; one correct component does not prove that the set is correct.
- Keep layer and function distinct. Do not convert bedding into curtains, upholstery, floor textiles, or generic fabric.
- Preserve each component's own edge stack, border, band, flange, inner seam, piping, binding, hem, cuff, opening, closure, tie, label, and corner construction.
- Never normalize different components into one generic piped or bordered edge.
- When a prototype shows a broad flat border or flange plus an inner seam and outer piping, require the full hierarchy rather than piping alone.
- Keep construction landmarks readable despite relaxed folds. Keep bedding clear of the floor unless floor contact is explicitly permitted.

### Blankets and throws

- Place a blanket only on description-approved furniture, such as a bed, sofa, lounge chair, armchair, or wicker chair.
- Present it as a removable, draped, folded, layered, or casually placed blanket; keep enough supporting furniture visible to prove that it is not upholstery.
- Keep a meaningful portion of the surface, thickness, reversible side when relevant, and edge construction visible.
- Keep it entirely off the floor unless floor contact is explicitly permitted.
- Never show it as a rug, runner, carpet, floor covering, upholstery, curtain, tablecloth, wall hanging, room divider, or slipcover unless that exact use is explicitly permitted.

### Soft-goods construction and color

- Preserve double-layer overlocked or serged edges wherever visible. The two edge layers may naturally close or separate in different areas, but must never be simplified into one generic edge.
- Preserve the prototype's apparent weave, nap, softness, thickness, weight, density, loft, drape, compression, and gravity response.
- For a color explicitly identified as rain-dew or natural linen, use warm unbleached natural linen or light wheat. Do not shift it toward grey, mushroom, cool taupe, or ivory.
- Keep defining pattern and construction landmarks readable despite natural folds.

## Cushions

- Preserve exact SKU inventory, requested quantity, dimensions, proportions, shape, seams, trim, and placement.
- Show only the requested quantity. Never duplicate a cushion merely to balance the composition.
- Treat square, rectangular, shaped, and sculptural cushions as distinct components rather than interchangeable props.

## Tray, vase, mineral stone, and branch compositions

Unless the description specifies otherwise, use exactly:

- one tray;
- one vase;
- one mineral stone;
- one branch emerging from the vase.

Do not add a second tray, second vase, second stone, candles, diffuser sticks, or competing product-like decor.

- Preserve the tray's exact shape, thickness, holes, edge profile, proportions, and material.
- Preserve the vase's exact silhouette, opening, wall thickness, base, proportions, finish, and handles when present.
- Preserve the mineral stone's visible form, color, translucency, grain, and surface behavior.
- Do not deform a floor lamp or any architectural object to accommodate the composition.
- Use restrained wabi-sabi floral styling only when it supports the product and does not obscure inventory or construction.

## Silk eye masks

- Use an approximate visible mask size of 15 x 10 cm unless supplied dimensions say otherwise.
- Preserve a thin, flexible two-silk-layer construction with no visible filling, padding, foam, eye cups, or invented piping unless explicitly confirmed.
- Use two independent ear loops, one per ear, like a surgical mask. Never substitute a headband or a single loop around the head.
- From a three-quarter view, the ear must hide the rear oval section. Never show a complete floating oval loop or a rear curve passing visibly around the ear.
- The visible upper strap travels from the upper mask edge toward the upper ear root and disappears behind the ear.
- The visible lower strap emerges below the earlobe and returns to the lower mask edge.
- Keep hair from obscuring the attachment logic when ear-loop geometry must be verified.
- Treat correct ear-loop geometry as a mandatory acceptance criterion, ahead of styling or scene beauty.

## Silk scrunchies

Preserve the supplied color, sheen, weave, fullness, seam placement, gathered geometry, and specified dimensions.

- Large scrunchie: approximately 6 cm wide, 30 momme silk.
- Small scrunchie: approximately 3.5 cm wide, 19 momme silk.
- Pink and purple small scrunchies remain the same size unless explicitly changed.

Do not make silk look plastic, metallic, velvet, satin-polyester, overstuffed, or limp. Do not invent charms, labels, crystals, hardware, or stitching.

For eye-mask and scrunchie imagery, use refined Place Vendome or high-jewelry still-life language only when requested. Use sophisticated Rembrandt-style light and aim for a credible EUR 200+ visual impression without adding jewelry-like embellishment to the product.

## Vases and other rigid decor

- Use only description-approved consoles, mantels, tables, niches, shelves, or plinths at plausible scale.
- Preserve silhouette, rim, opening, wall thickness, glaze or surface grain, handles, base, proportions, hardware, and material response.
- Add water, flowers, branches, or contents only when allowed by the description or by the tray/vase/stone default above.
- Keep competing vessels or sculptures visually subordinate and physically separate; omit them when they could be mistaken for another product unit.

## Tabletop and small decor

- Use an approved table, console, shelf, or still-life surface.
- Preserve exact product count, scale, construction, material, and contact behavior.
- Keep competing product-like props out of the frame.
